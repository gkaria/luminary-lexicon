# Further Reading on Steve Jobs

## Essential Biographies

### Steve Jobs by Walter Isaacson (2011)
The definitive biography, authorized by Jobs himself. Isaacson conducted over 40 interviews with Jobs over two years, as well as interviews with more than 100 family members, friends, colleagues, and competitors. This is the most comprehensive look at Jobs' life, from his childhood through his final days.

**Why read it:** Unparalleled access and depth. Jobs gave Isaacson complete freedom to write honestly, knowing he wouldn't see the manuscript before his death.

### Becoming Steve Jobs by Brent Schlender and Rick Tetzeli (2015)
A more nuanced portrait that challenges the narrative of Jobs as an unchanging tyrant. The authors knew Jobs for 25 years and argue that he evolved significantly as a leader, particularly after his time away from Apple.

**Why read it:** A counterpoint to Isaacson's biography, focusing on Jobs' growth and learning journey.

## On Apple's Culture and History

### Inside Apple by Adam Lashinsky (2012)
An investigation into how Apple operatesits culture of secrecy, its organizational structure, and what makes it tick.

**Why read it:** Understand the systems and culture Jobs built, which continue to shape Apple today.

### The Innovation Secrets of Steve Jobs by Carmine Gallo (2010)
Seven principles that drove Jobs' innovation: Do what you love, put a dent in the universe, kickstart your brain, sell dreams not products, say no to 1,000 things, create insanely great experiences, and master the message.

**Why read it:** Practical distillation of Jobs' innovation philosophy into actionable principles.

## On Design Philosophy

### Jony Ive: The Genius Behind Apple's Greatest Products by Leander Kahney (2013)
A biography of Apple's longtime Chief Design Officer, who was Jobs' closest collaborator on product design.

**Why read it:** Understand the design partnership that created the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad.

### Designed by Apple in California (2016)
Apple's own book, showcasing 20 years of Apple design. Limited text, but hundreds of photographs of Apple products.

**Why read it:** Visual feast that demonstrates Jobs' and Apple's obsession with every detail.

## Original Sources

### Stanford Commencement Address (2005)
Jobs' famous "Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish" speech. Available on YouTube and in transcript form.

**Why watch/read it:** Jobs in his own words, reflecting on connecting the dots, love and loss, and death.

### Steve Jobs: The Lost Interview (2012)
A 1995 interview with Robert Cringely, recorded shortly after Jobs left Apple and during his time at NeXT.

**Why watch it:** Jobs at a reflective moment, discussing what went wrong at Apple and his vision for the future.

### All Things Digital Interviews
Walt Mossberg and Kara Swisher's "D" conferences featured Jobs many times. These interviews show Jobs' thinking on technology trends, competition, and Apple's strategy.

**Why watch them:** See Jobs engaging with tough questions from knowledgeable journalists.

## Books by Jobs' Collaborators

### Creative Selection by Ken Kocienda (2018)
A memoir by a software engineer who worked on Safari and the original iPhone keyboard. Provides an insider's view of Apple's creative process.

**Why read it:** Ground-level view of how products actually got made at Apple under Jobs.

### The Ride of a Lifetime by Bob Iger (2019)
Disney CEO's memoir includes fascinating sections on negotiating with Jobs (Disney acquired Pixar) and Jobs' time on Disney's board.

**Why read it:** See Jobs from the perspective of a peer CEO who worked closely with him.

## On Pixar and Animation

### Creativity, Inc. by Ed Catmull (2014)
Pixar co-founder's book on building and sustaining a creative culture. Jobs bought Pixar from George Lucas and served as CEO until Disney acquired it.

**Why read it:** Jobs' influence extended beyond Apple. This shows how he helped build another transformative company.

### To Pixar and Beyond by Lawrence Levy (2016)
The story of Pixar's business transformation, from nearly bankrupt startup to a billion-dollar company, told by its CFO who was recruited by Jobs.

**Why read it:** Jobs the businessman and board chairman, not just the product visionary.

## On Technology and Design Philosophy

### The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman (1988)
While not about Jobs, this classic on user-centered design influenced the entire fieldand Jobs was deeply influenced by design thinking.

**Why read it:** Understand the design principles that shaped Jobs' approach to products.

### Insanely Simple by Ken Segall (2012)
Written by the ad executive who worked with Jobs and created the "Think Different" campaign. Explores Jobs' obsession with simplicity.

**Why read it:** Concrete examples of how Jobs' simplicity principle played out in marketing and product development.

## Documentaries

### Steve Jobs: The Man in the Machine (2015)
Alex Gibney's documentary examines both Jobs' brilliance and his darker side.

### Steve Jobs: One Last Thing (2011)
PBS documentary covering his entire career.

### General Magic (2018)
Documentary about the team that tried to invent the smartphone in the 1990sfeaturing many people who would later work with Jobs at Apple.

**Why watch:** Context for the innovation that led to the iPhone.

## For Academic Readers

### Steve Jobs and the NeXT Big Thing by Randall Stross (1993)
Contemporary account of Jobs during his "wilderness years" at NeXT.

**Why read it:** Jobs before his triumphant return to Appleshows his persistence through failure.
