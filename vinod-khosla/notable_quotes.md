# Vinod Khosla: Notable Quotes

## On Vision and Audacity

> "Pick a problem worth solving. Go after something that looks like a dream."

> "Be unreasonable. Reasonable people adapt to the world. Unreasonable people adapt the world to them."

> "The bigger the problem, the bigger the opportunity. Don't think incrementallythink exponentially."

> "We invest in zero-billion-dollar markets. If it's already a big market, we're too late."

## On Failure and Risk

> "My willingness to fail is what gives me the ability to succeed."

> "Failure doesn't matter, success does. Nobody remembers the failures along the wayonly the summit matters."

> "Try and fail, but don't fail to try."

> "If you're not willing to fail, you're not willing to succeed."

> "Most people and organizations are so afraid of failing that they never attempt the truly important ventures."

## On Experts and Conventional Wisdom

> "Experts are experts in a previous version of the world, not the world you're trying to create."

> "If you're doing something truly innovative, the experts will tell you it's impossible. That's when you know you're on the right track."

> "Dare to disagree with people who've done it before. They're often the worst advisors for doing it differently."

## On Strategy and Execution

> "Be obstinate about the vision but flexible about the tactics."

> "A company becomes the people it hires, not the plan it makes."

> "The first ten hires define your company forever. Choose carefullyyou're engineering your gene pool."

> "Focus on the consequences of success, not the probability of success."

> "Set up base camps on the way to Mt. Everest, but make sure they actually lead to the summit."

## On Technology and The Future

> "Technology is the only bridge to enable all 7 billion people to achieve a high standard of living."

> "AI is different in kind, not degree. It amplifies the human brain itself."

> "We're building a post-scarcity society. In 25-50 years, work will be optionalyou'll work because you want to, not because you have to."

> "AI will democratize expertise. One AI doctor can serve a billion people at near-zero marginal cost."

> "In two to three decades, what costs $50,000 in lifestyle today might cost $10,000."

## On Entrepreneurship

> "Don't chase what's hot. Build what's inevitable."

> "Incrementalism is a failure to try. You need order-of-magnitude improvements to change the world."

> "If everyone agrees with you, you're not doing something differentiated enough."

> "The best companies aren't built to compete. They're built to make competition irrelevant."

## On Hiring and Culture

> "I spent 40% of my time as a founder on recruiting. Every founder should do the same."

> "Hire people who have actually solved the specific problems you face, not just people with good resumes."

> "Build a culture where the most junior person can challenge the most senior executive."

> "Gene pool engineering is more important than strategy. The team makes thousands of daily decisions that determine your trajectory."

## On Persistence

> "With enough persistence, most things that seem impossible become possible."

> "I'm consistently amazed at how easily people accept 'no' and give up on their goals."

> "The difference between successful and unsuccessful entrepreneurs is often just persistence."

## On Education and Healthcare

> "College degrees will become obsolete and useless. Personalized AI tutors will provide better education than elite private schools."

> "AI will replace 80% of what doctors do. Healthcare will become nearly free and universally accessible."

> "A child in a remote village with an AI tutor will receive better education than a student at the most expensive private school."

> "Empathy will matter more than medical knowledge. Film school skills might be more valuable than a medical degree for caregivers."

## On Venture Capital and Investing

> "70-80% of VCs add negative value to companies. Most haven't earned the right to advise entrepreneurs."

> "I'll fund projects with a 90% chance of failure because the 10% success creates asymmetric value."

> "It's stories, not facts, that lure investors."

> "Traditional VCs are money managers. We're venture assistants. There's a big difference."

## On Climate and Sustainability

> "Climate change is a very solvable problem. Technology can create green solutions that are cheaper than dirty ones."

> "You don't need people motivated by climate concerns. If green is cheaper, economics drives adoption."

> "AI will turbocharge our ability to develop breakthrough climate technologies."

## On Decision Making

> "Seek out lots of alternative input, but maintain your internal compass."

> "The best founders synthesize their own unique path from diverse inputs. They don't just follow advice."

> "Listen to everyone. Then make your own decision. Don't outsource your strategic thinking."

## On Long-Term Thinking

> "Build for where the world is going, not where it is."

> "The transition to AI abundance will be painful and disruptive, but the destination is worth it."

> "Most dystopian AI fears are myopic, alarmist, and actually harmful. The real risks are misguided regulation and geopolitics."

## On Impact

> "The measure of success isn't the money you make. It's the dent you put in the universe."

> "We're not just building companies. We're inventing a radically different world."

> "Technology should free humanity from the burden of work and survival servitude."
