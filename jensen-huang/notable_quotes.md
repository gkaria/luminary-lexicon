# Jensen Huang: Notable Quotes

## On First-Principles Thinking

> "You can learn how something can be done and then go back to first principles and ask yourself, 'Given the conditions today& how would I reinvent this whole thing?'"

> "The way to think about this is to reason from first principles. Strip away all assumptions and ask: what's fundamentally true?"

## On Long-Term Vision

> "We pursue markets that don't exist yet. Zero-billion-dollar markets. Because if it's already a big market, we're too late."

> "Our job is to see around corners. We have to invent the future before others even see it's possible."

> "I'd rather invest ten years too early than one year too late."

## On Innovation and Risk

> "The riskiest thing you can do is play it safe. If you're not willing to fail, you're not willing to succeed."

> "Most companies die from indigestion, not starvation. They try to do too many things."

> "Do something that's very difficult. Because if it's easy, everybody else will do it."

## On Building for the Future

> "We don't build for today. We build for where the world is going."

> "CUDA was a 10-year investment before it paid off. But we knew it was foundational."

> "The best time to build infrastructure is before anyone knows they need it."

## On Leadership and Culture

> "Empower people to do things that seem impossible. That's when magic happens."

> "Hire people who are smarter than you and give them the freedom to prove it."

> "A company's culture is defined by the worst behavior its leaders are willing to tolerate."

## On Technology and AI

> "AI is the most powerful technology force of our time. It will reinvent every industry."

> "GPUs don't just accelerate computingthey enable computing that was previously impossible."

> "We're not in the chip business. We're in the AI infrastructure business."

## On Competition

> "Our competition isn't other chip companies. Our competition is the problem we're trying to solve not being solvable."

> "The goal isn't to beat the competition. The goal is to make the competition irrelevant by inventing new categories."

## On Failure and Resilience

> "NVIDIA has been on the brink of failure multiple times. Each time, we learned and came back stronger."

> "Retreat is not failure. Sometimes you need to step back to see the bigger picture."

> "The companies that survive are the ones that can reinvent themselves."

## On Execution

> "Strategy is important, but execution is everything. Ideas are cheap. Execution is priceless."

> "Speed matters. The faster you can iterate, the faster you can learn."

> "Perfect is the enemy of done. Ship and iterate."

## On Developers and Community

> "Developers are our customers, even if they don't buy directly from us. Win with developers, and everything else follows."

> "Make your tools free and your platform indispensable."

> "The best marketing is developers who love your platform."

## On the Future

> "In ten years, every industry will be an AI industry."

> "We're living through a fundamental transformation in computing. This doesn't happen oftenmaybe once every few decades."

> "The next great companies will be built on AI infrastructure. We're here to provide that foundation."

## On Decision Making

> "The hardest decisions aren't between good and bad. They're between good and good. You have to say no to good opportunities to say yes to great ones."

> "When everyone agrees, someone isn't thinking. Encourage dissent and debate."

> "Listen to your engineers. They see problemsand solutionsthat others miss."

## On Purpose and Impact

> "We're not just building chips. We're enabling scientific breakthroughs, advancing medicine, creating art, and accelerating human knowledge."

> "Technology should amplify human potential, not replace human purpose."

> "The measure of our success isn't stock price. It's the innovations we enable."

## Personal Philosophy

> "Stay humble. The moment you think you've figured it all out is the moment you become irrelevant."

> "Curiosity is the most important quality in a leader. Never stop learning."

> "Work should be challenging, meaningful, and fun. If it's not, you're doing it wrong."
